President

In a lot of ways, cooking is like an at home chemistry lab. I think that is why I enjoy it so much. Your recipe is like the lab instructions with your ingredients as the chemicals being used. If you stray from the instructions, you might end up with something horrible (ie. explosion, noxious gas, etc) or you might discover something wonderful and new. One of my goals in life is to write a recipe from scratch. Technically, you can create any baked goods as long as you know its correct flour:liquid:egg:fat:sugar ratio. Food can also bring people together. When my dad was little, his mom would make spice bars for him and his siblings every week. When I was at my grandma's house last Christmas, I made spice bars for my family to enjoy. My uncles and grandparents reminisced about their school days and coming home to the smell of nutmeg, cinnamon, and clove. My grandma especially enjoyed them. She missed be able to bake for her family, but was grateful she could still share and enjoy baked goods with them. Unfortunately, she passed away a few weeks after, but I am glad I was able to see her and give her a little bit of joy.
